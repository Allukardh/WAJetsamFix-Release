# Project sustainability

WAJetsamFix is independently developed and maintained in the maintainer's personal time.

The work goes far beyond writing a small tweak. It includes reverse engineering, device testing, jailbreak-specific integration, build infrastructure, package auditing, diagnostics, documentation, compatibility review and long-term maintenance as WhatsApp and jailbreak environments change.

That work consumes time that would otherwise belong to family, rest and paid responsibilities, and it also creates direct infrastructure, device-testing and electricity costs.

## Current public commitment

The current public RootHide Edition 0.7.1 package remains available under its documented **as-is** terms.

Everything beyond that stable release is new work, including:

- RootHide 0.8;
- configurable memory profiles;
- automatic peak-based recommendation;
- the conventional Dopamine Edition;
- future WhatsApp compatibility work;
- RootHide Bootstrap validation for future versions or new features;
- new diagnostics, recovery tools and documentation.

None of that work is owed, guaranteed or attached to a release schedule.

## Technical readiness and public publication are separate decisions

A private build may be technically successful, field-tested and close to release quality without automatically becoming public.

Public publication is a separate maintainer decision based on:

- technical quality and safety;
- expected support burden;
- available personal and family time;
- infrastructure and testing costs;
- the effort required to maintain the work after publication;
- whether continued public development is sustainable.

A private Alpha, roadmap item, completed milestone or future RC may remain private, be paused or be abandoned without creating an obligation to publish it.

## What sponsorship supports

Voluntary sponsorship helps offset:

- GitHub and build-infrastructure costs;
- device and jailbreak-environment testing;
- electricity and development overhead;
- time spent reviewing diagnostics and compatibility changes;
- the work required to turn private engineering into a documented public release.

Sponsorship makes continued public development more viable. It is recognition and support for the project, not a purchase contract.

## What sponsorship does not purchase

Sponsorship does **not** purchase:

- guaranteed or priority support;
- private troubleshooting;
- access to private builds;
- a particular feature;
- compatibility with a specific setup;
- influence over technical or safety decisions;
- a release date;
- a guarantee that a private edition will become public.

There is no sponsor threshold, countdown or pay-to-release promise.

## Publication model

The adopted model is:

> Keep development private and publish only when it is technically ready and sustainable.

Possible outcomes for future work include:

- continued private development;
- a documentation-only public update;
- a private RC;
- a controlled public RC;
- a stable public release;
- postponement or indefinite private retention.

The maintainer alone decides which outcome is appropriate for each edition and milestone.
